About this destination

Chappal Waddi — meaning "Mountain of Death" in Fulani — is Nigeria's highest peak at 2,419 metres. Located in Taraba State near the Cameroon border, within the Gashaka-Gumti National Park. The summit offers sweeping panoramic views of the Cameroon Highlands and the valley below. Rarely visited, almost never photographed. A true hidden gem.

Safety & what to expect

Always hire a local guide. The trail is unmarked and weather can change rapidly above 1,800m. Solo trekking is strongly discouraged.

Best visited November–February (dry season). Temperatures drop significantly at night — pack warm layers regardless of season.

Bring 3+ litres of water, high-energy snacks, a first aid kit, and trekking boots. No facilities on the mountain.
